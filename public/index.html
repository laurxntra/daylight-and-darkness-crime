As The Sun Rises Does Crime Fall?

When we picture a crime scene, we often think of abandoned alleyways, flickering street lights, and shrill police sirens echoing through the night sky. However, these crimes occur earlier than you expect!

As we can see above, in a month, there is not an overwhelming majority of nighttime crimes (from 8PM - 5 AM) across the various crime types in Phoenix, Arizona, with the number of daytime crimes (from 5 AM - 8 PM) for aggravated assault and motor-vehicle theft being not too far less than the number of nighttime crimes for those categories, and a significantly higher number of larceny-theft crimes occur in the daytime as opposed to the nighttime. In fact, the total daytime crimes for a month exceed the total nightime crimes!


Why?

In daytime, there are more people roaming the streets, which means more potential victims to commit various sorts of crimes, but in the night time there are less people roaming the streets and less visibility, which means a higher potential for violent crimes such as aggravated assault and motor-vehicle theft.
